OVERVIEW

The key research question: ‘How can we improve the Catan playing experience for players with different needs and skill levels?’

THE APPROACH

We have decided to design the app based on key issues that players face (in general and/or specific to Catan) when they play. This is done through two main methods; theoretical background research on hybrid-digital board game models and pre-existing board game assistant apps, as well as more practical user-based methods through interviewing players, and discussion within our research group.

THE GOAL

Our app aims to improve accessibility, simplify and enhance the playing experience of Catan. This can be done by offering translation options to make rules clearer for non-English speakers, simplifying resource management and playing experience through session tracking, and providing diverse game modes to cater for players of varying skill levels.

THE PROTOTYPE

CONNECT WITH PLAYERS & VIEW A SHARED MAP

A simple homescreen layout with two clear actions: Start game or load a saved game. Starting a new game allows players to connect with each other through a unique game code, and view a shared map of the board on their devices. Players can also choose to load a saved game, allowing them to pause and resume games at their convenience.

ACCESS RULEBOOK WITHIN CLICKS

Players no longer need to manually flip through rulebook pages. Search key words in the rulebook to quickly find the information you need. Filtering options available specific game components.

EVERYTHING YOU NEED TO KNOW IN ONE SCREEN

Simply expand a game component to view summarized information instantly. Save playing time.

A FEW ADDITIONAL FEATURES

Resource management screen to include icons that mirror the symbology of the physical board game to enhance cohesion.

Victory points page that tracks players' houses & points, removing need for players to calculate manually.

Dice rolling feature and timer for players who wish to play in small sessions.

NEXT STEPS


My next step is to gather user evaluation results and further experiment with design options and further refining features. Some features to consider from user testing:


Inconsistency in colour scheme:
Some participants have expressed confusion on the transition of bright red on the homepage to a beige brown colour once they have left the homepage (started game). Some described the process of hosting/joining a game to be dull, as the host/join game page is simply a page with two buttons in the centre without anything going on in the background besides solid red. A few participants had preferences for modern styled apps and therefore did not enjoy the medieval themed prototype.


Ambiguous icon meaning:
While many participants understood metaphorical icons, a few icons were misinterpreted by users. The trophy icon for example corresponds to the victory points page in the context of our app, but some participants thought it was a leaderboard page, which is common in other apps.
